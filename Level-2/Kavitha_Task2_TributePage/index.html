Tribute to a Pioneer

Marie Curie

Physicist, chemist, and two-time Nobel Prize winner who paved the way for modern science and medical imaging.

Early Life

Born Maria Skłodowska in Warsaw in 1867, she moved to Paris to continue her education and began groundbreaking research in radioactivity.

Achievements

  • First woman to win a Nobel Prize.
  • Only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different sciences (Physics and Chemistry).
  • Co-discovered the elements polonium and radium.

Legacy

Her discoveries led to advanced cancer treatments and the use of X-rays in medicine, inspiring generations of women in science.

Timeline

1898

Announced discovery of polonium and radium.

1903

Won Nobel Prize in Physics with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel.

1911

Received Nobel Prize in Chemistry for radium research.
